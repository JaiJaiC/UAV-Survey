Abst ract- Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) 
has been extensiv ely applied to solve the problem of robot 
localization and mapping without absolute position measure­
ments. Visual sensors are widely used in odometry techniques 
due to their low cost and richer information content. How­
ever,when robots operate far from the scene (e.g., high-altitude 
UAVs), visual-inertial odometry (VIO) positioning accuracy de­
grades significantly. This compromises the balance between au­
tonomous precision and rapid scene understanding. To address 
this issue, we propose a collaborative dual-U AV localization and 
real-time scene reconstruction framework for high-altitude op­
erations.By utilizing inter-U AV visual overlap observations and 
wireless ranging constraints, we propose a collaborati ve decen­
tralized trajectory localization and global map point consistency 
optimization scheme. Our solution achieves high-altitude precise 
positioning and maintains dual-U AV observation consistency, 
enabling real-time construction of large-scale naviga ble maps. 
Evaluation results in photorealistic simulated environments 
demonstrate the superior accuracy of our approach in high­
altitude localization and scene reconstruction. 
I. INTRODU CTION 
Autonomous navigation is essential for unmanned systems 
to perform complex tasks. It requires two key techno logies : 
prec ise loca lization and environmental mappin g. When op­
erating in unknown environ ments. unmanned aerial vehicles 
(UAV s) must instantaneously and accurately estimate their 
positions while perceiving surrounding structures to enable 
real-time path planning and collision avoid ance. Although 
GNSS (e.g .. GPS/R TK) provides reliable positioning in open 
areas [l]. signal degradation and loca lization drift occu r in 
high -altitude. indoor. or com plex environments [2]. More­
over. GNSS systems lack the capability to provide 3D 
environmental geometry. further com plicating autonomous 
navigation in cluttered or GPS-denied spaces. 
To overcome these limitat ions. visual SLAM (Sim ulta­
neous Loca lization and Mappi ng) has emerged as a viable 
solution. By leve raging onboard cameras and sensors. visual 
SLAM enables GPS-denied autonomous loca lization and 
environmental mapping [3]. In recent years. visual-i nertial 
odometry (VIO) SLAM technique that fuses visual and 
inertial data has become the de facto standard for UAV 
navigation due to its cost-efficiency. low power consumption.

in high-a ltitude flight scenar ios. we prop ose an optimized 
method using a dual -UA V system. The cooperative effect 
between two peer UAV s is utilized to increase the baseline 
length between them. thereby optimizing triangulation angles 
and significantly improving positioning accuracy. Simultane­
ously. by fusing point cloud data collected from the expan ded 
obse rvation ranges of both UAVs. more com plete and de­
tailed scene mesh maps can be rapidly generated. thereby 
providing more reliable references for unmanned system 
navigation and obstacle avoidance as shown in Fig. 1. This

II. RELATED WO RK 
Multi -robot SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Map­
ping) has emerged as a prominent research focus, with its 
core objective being colla borative environment mappi ng and 
self-localization among multiple robots [7]. To addr ess the 
degradation of VIO perf ormance at high altitud es, prior work 
[8] demon strated significant VIO results at elevati ons up 
to 50 mete rs. Alternative approaches employ dual-camera 
setups mounted at opposite ends of fixed-wing aircraft to 
extend the baseline of virtual stereo cameras [9][10], though 
such methods remain cons trained by the physical dimen sions 
of the airframe. Studies in [11] [12] utilize two independent 
UAV s to con struct shared maps with information consi stency 
constraints, effectively enhancing both localization accuracy 
and mapping efficiency in multi-robot systems. However, 
in complex environments , inter-robot communication often 
proves unstable, prompting inves tigations in [13] [14] into 
distr ibuted filtering algorithms that enable efficient data 
exchange and cooperative localization under limited com­
munication bandwidth. 
Point cloud-based scene reconstruction has gained 
widesp read adoption in robotic navigation due to its ability to 
provide precise geometric representations of envi ronme nts. 
Recent advances in Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and 
3D Gaussian Splatting [15][16] have sign ificantly improved 
reconstruction quality, though their high computational costs 
pose challenges for real-time implementati on. [17] pion eered 
real-time semantic mesh recon structi on, while [18] extended 
this capab ility to multi-robot syste ms, albeit with notable 
scale drift issues in large-scale envi ronme nts. The [19] 
framework introduced an innovative incremental mesh recon­
struction appr oach using LiDAR sensors, achieving substan­
tial improvements in both reconst ruction speed and accuracy 
across exten sive areas. 
Inspired by [12] , we employ a dual-drone syst em relying 
solely on camera sensors (without LiDAR) to achieve precise localization in high-a ltitude areas. By levera ging visual co­
obse rvation overlap constraints for map consist ency, our ap­
proach enables faster and more accurate large-scale mapping 
compared to single-d rone low-altitude map ping. 
III. METHOD 
A. Sys tem Overview 
The distri buted SLAM system is implemented on two 
identical UAV s that communicate via radio links . As shown 
in Fig.3 (execu tion mod ule of one UAV), each drone's 
functionality is divided into two core modul es: (1) Localiza­
tion Mod ule -primarily consisting of MSCKF-based sensor 
fusion positioning and inter-U AV ADMM optimiza tion; (2) 
Map ping Module. 
Localization Module: The localization module is respon­
sible for estimating the initial pose of each image frame by 
fusing data from inertial sens ors and camera senso rs using 
an Extended Kalman Filter (EKF). It selects repre sentative 
images over a peri od of time as keyframes (KFs ); Upon 
obtaining the poses of these keyf rames and their observed 
map points, an optimization thread is initiated. This thread 
perf orms map point matc hing in the co-visible areas between 
the two agents . The optimization incorporates the current 
keyf rame information from the local agent, the latest received 
keyf rame information from the other UAV, UWB ranging 
data, and co-visi bility information to refine both frame poses 
and map point locat ions . The optimized pose devia tions serve 
as pseudo -obse rvati ons for the EKF to correct trajectory 
position s. 
Mapping Module: In the map ping module, the consist ent 
camera poses and map point positions stored by both UAV s 
are used as input. To enable incremental map ping in large­
scale envi ronmen ts, four data types are employed to represent 
differe nt scene structur es : mesh vertices , triangular faces, 
region s, and voxels. Two data structure s(a hash table and an 
octree)are used for data storage and proces sing. The global 
mesh map is cons tructed through dimensi onality reduction 
proj ection of verti ces, Delaunay triangulation and spatial 
inver se transformati on.

For each vertex Vii E Oi, search for vertices within a radius 
dr of it. 
Vertices found outside Oi are merged into the voxel 's ver­

1) Spatial Mesh Partitioning 
Performing mesh partiti oning directly among 3D vertices 
in space is highly chall enging. Theref ore, it is necessa ry to 
proj ect the 3D vertices onto a 2D plane for mesh part itioning.

characte ristics preserved in the 2D space, resulting in lower­
quality recons tructed maps . For vertices within a smalle r re­
gion, they tend to fit onto a plane, and the mesh partiti oned on 
this plane, when reproj ected into 3D space, retains its mesh 
properties as much as possible.Si milar to other approac hes, 
we apply Delaunay triangulation to vertices proj ected onto a 
2D planes well-established method exten sively documented 
in [17].

V. CONCLUSIONS 
In this work, we propose a novel framework for au­
tonomous UAV localization and grid-based mappi ng in high­
altitude scenari os. Our appr oach levera ges longer baseline 
stereo cons traints and wireless communicati on-based ranging 
between two UAV s to optimize their relative poses and 
constr uct a globally consi stent map . The syst em adopts a de­
centralized optimization strategy, where each UAV indepen­
dently estimates its trajectory and builds a local map while 
exch anging key information to maintain global consist ency.
